Practice Exercise · CRM Administration

HubSpot user provisioning & role-based permission mapping

A self-directed HubSpot practice exercise built around a realistic brief: inviting new hires to a HubSpot portal with permissions scoped precisely to department and seniority, rather than default role templates. First in a small series of hands-on HubSpot administration exercises.

Scenario · Company scaling its sales & marketing team, 5 new hires Method · Permission mapping, per-user access configuration Tools · HubSpot Settings, CRM object & tool permissions
Brief

Not a simple bulk invite

The brief: a company scaling its sales and marketing team needed 5 new hires added to HubSpot. The catch was that each user needed permissions scoped specifically to their department and seniority level, governed by a permission mapping key rather than a single default role.

Requirements

  • Invite all 5 users via the provided email list
  • Assign each to the correct team in HubSpot
  • Configure permission sets per the attached mapping, not a blanket template
  • Confirm every invite reaches "Pending" before sign-off
Permission Mapping Key

Access rules by role

Sales Representative

Paid Sales Hub seat

View/edit own contacts only. No export rights.

Sales Manager

Paid Sales Hub seat

View/edit the full team's contacts. Bulk-delete permitted.

Marketing (Content)

Free seat

Full access to Blogs, Landing Pages, and Forms. No access to Settings or Billing.

Operations

Free seat

Super Admin — full access to all tools and workflows.

Approach

Configuring access per user, not per template

01

Read the mapping key

Translated each role's seat type and access rule into a checklist before touching HubSpot's UI.

02

Walk the invite flow

Worked through HubSpot's Email → Access → Review flow individually for each of the 5 users.

03

Set object-level access

Scoped Contacts permissions (view, create, edit, delete, merge) to owned vs. unassigned records per role, not a default template.

04

Lock down CRM tools

Switched off bulk delete, import, and export where the mapping key required it, enforcing "no export rights" precisely.

Execution

Configuring the Sales Representative profile

The permission set applied to one of the Sales Representative invites, translated from the mapping key into HubSpot's object and tool-level access controls.

Contacts (object access)

  • View Owned + unassigned
  • Create Enabled
  • Edit Owned + unassigned
  • Delete Owned only
  • Merge Owned only

CRM tools

  • Bulk delete Off
  • Import Off
  • Export Off
  • Workflows Off
  • Reports View only

Result

All 5 users provisioned with role-correct access: no one was over-permissioned (representatives can view and edit their own contacts but can't bulk-delete or export data), and seat types matched actual license needs rather than defaulting everyone to a paid seat, avoiding paying for Sales Hub access where a Free seat covered Marketing and Operations.
